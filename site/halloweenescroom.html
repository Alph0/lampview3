Halloween Escape Room

Description:

A halloween themed escape room.

Goal:

An escape room designed to be beat in ~20 minutes by visitors of Engineering Tech open house at Monty Tech.

Elements:

- A shelf where the player needs to place books with letters on the spines into empty spots according to their letters to spell a word.

- Invisible ink on the wall that the player can see with an ultraviolet flashlight found in the room.

- A resistor which the player can derive a code from by using a resistor color code poster.

- Medicine that is labeled "screaming medicine" with a name on it hinting towards the player that they need to yell the name to get a code.

- PLC controlled module with 3 buttons on it where each button corresponds to a number in a code the player has to find.

This project was designed from a flowchart we designed first and foremost, and then the stages were distributed among Lampview "employees".

My [Jonathan Lefferts'] part in the project was the bookshelf and the electronic doorknob cover.

Bookshelf

Inside each of the books there is some tape on the page side, one of the tape pieces have a piece of metal inside.

Each book corresponded to a letter, and each letter had a different spot where the metal would be.

This allows me to place inductive sensors on the back of the shelf that align with their specific book.

Each of these sensors communicate with a PLC, which governs a motor drive with a key attached to a chain.

Once all of the inductive sensors are activated, the motor drive spins until a contact is pressed with a physical piece on the chain.

All of this allows for the player to spell the word "ATOM" and then get their key automatically.

Door Cover

A problem we ecountered was making sure people have a concrete ending to the puzzle, like they would get a key and escape for example, without damaging our door or locking people inside a room.

Other escape rooms have this problem too, since they just can't lock people in a room, what if there is a fire?

So what they do is they just make a light turn green and then you can leave... boring.

My idea was to make a lightweight door cover which sticks onto the door with magnets so we don't have to damage our door, and then incorporate it into the security system box.

If a person wants to get out in case of a fire or other danger, I used a small relatively weak motor so the player can just push it aside and open the door, but also it gives a satisfying ending to the room.

Results

Shockingly, everything worked come open house!

But,

it was a lot harder than we anticipated, people finished in a far longer time than we thought. We only had time for like, 2 groups.

We'd rather have it be harder than easier to be honest. And it seemed like a good time for the groups that participated as well!
